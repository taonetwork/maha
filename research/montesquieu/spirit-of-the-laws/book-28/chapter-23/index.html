Chapter 23: The Code of Laws on judicial Combats

.

March 10, 2020

SOME perhaps will have a curiosity to see this abominable custom of judiciary combat reduced to principle, and to find a code of such extraordinary laws.

Men, though reasonable in the main, reduce their very prejudices to rule. Nothing was more contrary to good sense, than those combats: Yet when once this point was laid down, a kind of prudential management was used in carrying it into execution.

In order to be thoroughly acquainted with the jurisprudence of those times, it is necessary to read with attention the regulations of St. Lewis, who made such great changes in the judiciary order.
