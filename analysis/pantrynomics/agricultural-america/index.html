An Agricultural America

The Vision of Adam Smith

October 17, 2019

In Book 3 of the Wealth of Nations, Adam Smith advocated that agricultural countries should max out their capital on agriculture first before investing it on industry, specifically to build ships and logistics technology. He gives example of Egypt, India, and China which were technologically backward relative to Rome and Europe.

This implies that Smith preferred the American colonies to stay backward in the short term and source its technology from Britain. The demand of America for technology would then spur the industry of its mother country and help it keep its edge over its rivals such as France and Germany.

This is in line with Thomas Jefferson’s plan for an agricultural America. Unfortunately, this was defeated by Hamilton’s industrial policies which went against Smith’s advocacy.

So now, you have an American industrial power with liberal morals instead of the traditional British morals. This manifests as the USA invading countries for oil and resources and setting up corrupt dictators. This is opposite of the British who installed proper administrators like Raffles in Singapore and MacLehose in Hongkong to lead those countries to prosperity.

In effect, the US became a huge mercantile company (now known as neo-colonialism) that installed corrupt dictators like Reza Shah, Ngo Diem, and Ferdinand Marcos who destroyed Iran, South Vietnam, and the Philippines while providing debt revenue to American banks and raw materials for US manufacturers.

People mistakenly assume that the current capitalist system was the one advocated by Smith for America. In fact, it’s the opposite–Smith was very much against capitalism, then known as mercantilism.

The rural white America is the natural dharma of the US and this is currently seen in the oppression against blacks. This then implies that the policies of Lincoln and Hamilton came from mercantilism and not from an enlightened understanding of things.
