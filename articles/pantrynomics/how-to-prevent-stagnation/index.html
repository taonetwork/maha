how to prevent stagnation

“In a country fully peopled.. the competition for employment would necessarily be so great as to reduce the wages of labour to what was barely sufficient to keep up the number of labourers, and, the country being already fully peopled, that number could never be augmented. In a country fully stocked in proportion to all the business it had to transact, as great a quantity of stock would be employed.. as the nature and extent of the trade would admit. The competition.. would everywhere be as great, and consequently the ordinary profit as low as possible. But perhaps no country has ever yet arrived at this degree of opulence. China seems to have been long stationary, and had probably long ago acquired that full complement of riches which is consistent with the nature of its laws and institutions. But this complement may be much inferior to what, with other laws and institutions, the nature of its soil, climate, and situation might admit of.”

His solutions were the same as his general advocacies against the mercantile system:

Ban lobbying by vested interests. This will prevent monopoly which prevents the circulation of goods and services (Book 1, Chap 11)
Ban trading of securities by the general public. This will prevent stock bubbles or the loss of the people's saved-up revenue. IPOs are still allowed though, so that companies can have access to funds. (Lecures, Chap 3)
Zero income taxes on wages. This ensures the economy will run. (Book 5, Chap 2)
Living wages instead of a nominal minimum wage. This also ensures a running, non-stagnating economy. (Book 1, Chap 10)
Limiting of Intellectual Property to a shorter period. This ensures that new things and technologies can be produced, limiting stagnation (Book 5, Chap 1)
Governments must be frugal. This would prevent budget deficits and ensures the government always has money to spend (Book 5, Chap 3)
